Te Whare Tapa Whā

“Wellness encompasses a healthy body, a sound mind, and a tranquil spirit. Enjoy the journey as you strive for wellness.”
- Laurette Gagnon Beaulieu

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) :

Every week I'd aim to go for at least 2 badminton sessions on the weekend. Usually about 2 to 3 hours each session. Badminton involves a lot of quick decision making in order to win a point, so at the same time it trains my brain too.



Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) :

Well, I have no family in New Zealand but I do have a few close friends. I don't usually go hangout with them, only once in a while. But when we do, it's a lot of fun and good quality time together. They totally understand that my social battery runs out quick.



Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) :

I personally prefers A LOT of me-time. When I say a lot, it's like even my housemate will not be able to see me for more than 48 hours even though I am at home. I love the tranquil and quietness, having my Youtube music on "Natural Rainforest Sound" which kinda creates that soothing environment to relax my mind.



Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) :

I personally do not deny the existence of God. I am a buddhist, more precisely, I came from a buddhist family. But I personally don't practice it, I believed in only my personal hardwork as to survive in the real world. But I respect all kind of religion beliefs. It's more about keeping a good heart and having a kind soul.



Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) :

This is propably the hardest for me. I like to spend time in something that will give results. Subconciously, I might feel like taking a walk or spending hours out on a walk is taxing too much on my time to be productive. I treat that as a reward when the tasks on my hand are all cleared then I'd go out and take walks. However, I do enjoy the views of nature and like the peace that it has brought to me.
