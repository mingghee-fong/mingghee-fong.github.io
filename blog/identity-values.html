Identity, Values & Strengths

Story That Made Me Who I am Today.

Ethical Decision:

This is when I was 18 years old, right after high school. My parents had sent me to university, but not long after I became very homesick and decided to quit after the first semester due to several panic attack *at the time I thought I was sick, only found out it was panic attack last year after seeing someone was suffering from it*. So I had no choice but to come back to my hometown.

Unfortunately, I had missed the entry date for the local government pre-university. Unbeknownst to me, my mom went to the school to beg the principal for late admission. However, I didn't appreciate the effort she had put in, and I didn't study hard. As a result, I failed an important exam, and my mom cried, asking why I hadn't tried my best. At that moment, I realized I needed to take responsibility for my education and make a change. So, I decided to move out of my home and for myself to be more independent.

After that event, I made a significant ethical decision related to my education by enrolling in a Culinary School to pursue my passion as a chef. This decision was influenced by the values of independence and hard work instilled in me by my family.

Looking back, I realize the ethical dimension of my decision. It involved valuing my education and appreciating the sacrifices my parents were making for me. I weighed my desire for independence against my responsibility to make the most of the educational opportunities provided.

Ultimately, I chose to take control of my education and my life. Now, reflecting on that decision, I am proud of myself for stepping out of my comfort zone, pushing my limits, and appreciating the importance of education and family support.


Influence of Culture and People:

My values and identity have been significantly influenced by my family's culture and their life experiences. My parents had challenging upbringings, and they have always strived to provide my sister and me with the best opportunities.

They emphasized the importance of independence, a value that became more apparent to me when I left home. Witnessing their resilience and determination in the face of adversity has shaped my values of hardwork, self-reliance, and empathy for others.

My family's culture has taught me the value of perseverance and the importance of supporting one another.


Strengths for My Learning Journey:

One of my strengths that will support me on my learning journey is the ability to approach matters in a non-biased manner. This means I can assess situations from multiple angles and viewpoints, which is crucial for problem-solving and decision-making.

Additionally, I am comfortable working independently, which allows me to take initiative and be self-motivated in my learning and career development.


Limitations and Their Impact:

However, I also recognize my limitations. I am not naturally inclined to be people-oriented, and I often struggle to seek help or collaborate effectively with others. This could be a potential obstacle on my learning journey, as learning often involves teamwork, communication, and seeking guidance from others. My introverted tendencies might hinder my ability to network and build relationships crucial for career development.


Working with Others:

In the past, I have faced a situation where tension arose while working with others in the kitchen. Different opinions clashed, and there was resistance to finding a solution. To address this, we tried a strategy that involved putting egos aside and promoting open-minded communication.

This approach, though challenging, was effective in diffusing tension and allowing us to collaborate more effectively. Reflecting on this experience, I realize that while this strategy worked to some extent, it's also essential to acknowledge and respect diverse perspectives from the beginning.

In future situations, I would focus on fostering a more inclusive and collaborative environment from the start, encouraging everyone to contribute their ideas and actively seeking consensus to avoid unnecessary tension.
