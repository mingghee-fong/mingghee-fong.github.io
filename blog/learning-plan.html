My Learning Plan

— Henry Ford

For the time being, I don't have a long term goal. I always focus on my short term goal and then build my lego up. Right now, my short term goal is to be able to finish this Dev bootcamp and then land on a job as a junior developer or junior programmer.


My Strengths & Limitations:

Strengths:

Quick Learning and Adaptability:
Strong ability to learn quickly, adapt to new situations, and excel in tasks through visual learning.

Analytical Skills:
Strong analytical skills, ability to break down complex issues and provide valuable insights.

Self-Motivation:
Strong drive and motivation to succeed. Willingness to take on challenges and not give up easily. Determination to persevere, even when faced with setbacks.

Self-Awareness:
Demonstrating self-awareness by recognizing your strengths, limitations, and areas where improvement is needed.


Limitations:

Emotional Control:
Can become upset easily and having intense mood swings. Getting overly frustrated particularly in situations beyond my control.

Time Management:
Struggle with time management, often becoming too absorbed in tasks and not allocating time efficiently.

Communication:
Dislike unnecessary communication and avoidance of interactions with others.

Perfectionism:
Pursuit of perfection can lead to stress and a reluctance to accept mistakes or delegate tasks to others.


Non-technical Core Skills & Human Skills Deveplopment:

Emotional Intelligence:
Enhancing my emotional intelligence to help manage my emotions better, as well as navigate interpersonal relationships more effectively.

Communication Skills:
Improving my communication skills to make myself a more effective communicator, both in written and spoken forms. This includes active listening, public speaking, and the ability to convey ideas clearly and persuasively.

Conflict Resolution:
Learning how to resolve conflicts and disagreements in a constructive manner.

Time Management:
Enhancing my time management skills to improve my ability on prioritizing tasks, meet deadlines, and achieve a better work-life balance.

Networking and Relationship Building:
Building a strong professional network and developing relationship-building skills that can help me connect with others, maintain relationships, and leverage my network effectively.

Flexibility and Open-Mindedness:
Cultivating a flexible and open-minded attitude can enhance my ability to consider different perspectives and adapt to new ideas and approaches.


Commitment On Managing My Workload In This Programme:

How I Will Try To Manage Myself To Work Productively & Safely With Others:

Effective Communication:
I will actively work on improving my communication skills, especially in terms of active listening and expressing myself clearly and respectfully. This will help me collaborate more effectively with fellow learners, facilitators, and industry/community representatives.

Emotional Intelligence:
I will continue to develop my emotional intelligence, particularly in managing my own emotions and understanding the emotions of others. This will help me build stronger relationships and resolve conflicts more constructively.

Adaptability:
Building on my natural adaptability, I will remain open to different working styles and ideas from my peers and facilitators. I'll embrace change when necessary and demonstrate flexibility in group projects and tasks.

Stress Management:
Given my tendency to experience stress and frustration, I will proactively work on stress management techniques, such as mindfulness, time management, and seeking support when needed. This will ensure I can maintain emotional balance even during demanding periods.

What I Will Do When Things Are Building Up & Starting to Get to Me:

Recognize Early Warning Signs:
I will pay attention to early signs of stress and overwhelm, such as changes in mood, decreased productivity, or physical symptoms. Self-awareness will be my first line of defense.

Time Management:
I will refine my time management skills to ensure I allocate sufficient time for assignments, projects, and personal well-being. This proactive approach will help prevent last-minute rushes.

Reach Out for Support:
When I feel that things are starting to get overwhelming, I will try not to hesitate when it comes to asking for support. This could include discussing my concerns with facilitators or fellow learners.

Adapt and Adjust:
If necessary, I will be prepared to adjust my workload or priorities. This might involve reprioritizing assignments, or even taking a short break to recharge when needed.

Practice Self-Care:
I will prioritize self-care routines, including regular exercise, healthy eating, and sufficient sleep. These practices will help me maintain physical and mental well-being, making it easier to handle stress.

Reflect and Learn:
After challenging periods, I will take time to reflect on what caused the stress and how I can better manage similar situations in the future. Learning from these experiences will contribute to my personal growth.


Commintment On Seeking Help In a Timely Manner:

I am committed to seeking help promptly when needed during the program. I will stay self-aware, communicate clearly, build a support network, seek mentorship, use available resources, manage my time effectively, access counseling services when necessary, collaborate with peers, and learn from my experiences. This commitment ensures that I address challenges promptly and make the most of my learning journey.


Expectation From The Facilitation Team:

I think sometimes I will ask dumb questions or even when examples/explanations given to me, I may not get it still. I just hope the facilitation team will be patient with me because I will be frustrated with myself for not understand it too. Sigh.


Scheduling Information:
